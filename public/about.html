CDRH Standards & Regulations

In the United States, laser products and laser light shows are regulated by the FDA's Center for Devices and Radiological Health (CDRH). CDRH administers the Federal Performance Standard for Laser Products under 21 CFR Part 1040, which sets requirements for laser classification, labeling, and manufacturer reporting.

Entertainment laser shows that project into audience areas typically require an FDA Laser Light Show Variance — a CDRH-approved exception demonstrating that our safety procedures, equipment, and show design protect the public. LaserGator operates under an approved variance; every laser operator on our team holds LSO certification.

  • Compliance with applicable CDRH performance standards and variance conditions
  • All laser operators hold LSO certifications and comply with CDRH and ANSI laser safety standards
  • Pre-show venue review, beam path analysis, and audience separation where required
  • Alignment with ILDA and ANSI laser safety best practices

Official guidance: FDA CDRH — Laser Light Shows · 21 CFR Part 1040
